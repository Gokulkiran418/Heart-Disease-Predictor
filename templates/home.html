Facts About Heart Disease

Global Impact

Heart disease is the leading cause of death worldwide, claiming over 17 million lives annually, according to the WHO.

Risk Factors

High blood pressure, high cholesterol, and smoking are major risk factors, contributing to 80% of heart disease cases.

Prevention

Up to 80% of heart disease cases can be prevented through lifestyle changes like healthy eating and regular exercise.

Tips for a Healthy Heart

Include fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats (like omega-3s from fish) to support heart health.

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity (e.g., brisk walking) or 75 minutes of vigorous activity weekly.

Practice mindfulness, meditation, or yoga to reduce stress, which can elevate blood pressure and heart disease risk.

Habits to Avoid for Heart Health

  • Smoking: Smoking damages blood vessels and reduces oxygen supply, increasing heart attack risk.
  • Excessive Alcohol: Limit alcohol to one drink per day for women and two for men to avoid heart strain.
  • Sedentary Lifestyle: Avoid prolonged sitting; take breaks to move every 30 minutes to improve circulation.
  • High-Sugar Diets: Excess sugar can lead to obesity and diabetes, both linked to heart disease.
